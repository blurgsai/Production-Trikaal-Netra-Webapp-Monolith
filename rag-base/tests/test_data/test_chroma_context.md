## The Aurelia Star-Weaver

The **Aurelia Star-Weaver** (scientific name: *Aurelia astrotecta*) is a highly unusual, bioluminescent siphonophore-like organism discovered in the deep sub-surface oceans of **Kepler-186f**. 

### Key Characteristics:
1. **Electromagnetic Threading**: Unlike terrestrial marine organisms, the Star-Weaver generates micro-fine electromagnetic filaments from its secondary tentacles. It uses these threads to construct intricate, lattice-like structures around geothermal vents.
2. **Thermal Anchoring**: These woven lattices stabilize the temperature gradients around the vents, creating a localized micro-climate that shelters secondary symbiotic micro-organisms.
3. **Spectral Luminescence**: It emits a distinct pulsed light at a frequency of 485 THz (a deep cyan color) to communicate coordinates and resource density within its colony.
